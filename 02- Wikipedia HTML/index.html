Born 14 April 1891, Mhow, India

Profession Jurist, Economist, Politician
Known For Architect of Indian Constitution

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar was an Indian jurist, economist, social reformer, and political leader who played a crucial role in drafting the Constitution of India. He is widely regarded as the principal architect of the Indian Constitution and a champion of social justice.

Throughout his life, Ambedkar worked against social discrimination and fought for the rights of marginalized communities. His contributions continue to shape modern India.

Early Life

Dr. Ambedkar was born on 14 April 1891 in Mhow, Madhya Pradesh. He was born into a socially disadvantaged community and faced discrimination from a very young age.

Despite these challenges, he showed exceptional academic talent and determination, which laid the foundation for his future achievements.

Education

Ambedkar pursued higher education abroad, which was rare during his time. He studied at Columbia University in the United States and later at the London School of Economics.

His education equipped him with deep knowledge in economics, law, and political science, which he later used to shape India’s governance system.

Career and Contributions

Dr. Ambedkar served as India's first Law Minister and chaired the Drafting Committee of the Constitution. He played a key role in ensuring equality, liberty, and justice were embedded into the legal framework of India.

He also led movements against caste discrimination and advocated for the rights of women and laborers.

Life Timeline
